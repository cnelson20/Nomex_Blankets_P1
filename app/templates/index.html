Checkers!

Checkers, also known as Draughts, is a group of strategy board games for two players which involve diagonal moves of uniform game pieces and mandatory captures by jumping over opponent pieces.

Rules

Checkers is played by two players, on opposite sides of an 8x8 checkered gameboard. Only the dark squares of the checkered board are used. One player has the dark pieces; the other has the light pieces. Players alternate turns moving their own pieces. A move consists of moving a piece diagonally to an adjacent unoccupied square. If the adjacent square contains an opponent's piece, and the square immediately beyond it is vacant, the piece may be captured (and removed from the game) by jumping over it into the vacant square. A piece may move only diagonally into an unoccupied square. Only one piece may be captured with a single jump, but multiple jumps are allowed in a single turn. When presented, capturing is mandatory in most official rules and where the player does not capture the opposing player can remove the opponent piece as penalty and where there are two or more such positions the player forfeits that/those he cannot move. Although some rule variations make capturing optional. For our variation, capturing is optional, but if you capture once and can jump again you must jump again. If a player loses all their pieces or is unable to move a piece because they are blocked they lose.

How to Play

Click on the unit you want to move and then the space you want to move it to. You will only be allowed to select two spots. If the move is invalid nothing will happen and you will have to reselect.
